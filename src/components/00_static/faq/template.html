General questions

Is Lives in Transit a commercial or for-profit project?

No, Lives in Transit is entirely non-commercial, not-for-profit. It has been developed as part of a publicly-funded academic research project led by the University of Zurich (see "Partners" tab).

Where is the line between fact and fiction in Lives in Transit?

(a) All of the documents (primary and secondary sources) that you see in Lives in Transit are real. So are the archives and public institutions in which you collect those sources. (b) However, your "home" institutions, including your university and local museums, are fictional, as are all the characters that appear in the game. All of the interactions you have in the game are also fictional and the product of the authors' imagination. Thus, in your interactions with fictional characters, any resemblance to actual persons, living or dead, events, or locales is entirely coincidental.

What is the software behind Lives in Transit?

The software programme has been custom-built for this project by our external partners, and is completely open source. The programme is called "Marugoto" (meaning "the whole" in Japanese). For information about how you can contribute to Lives in Transit, please see the "About" tab.

Troubleshooting

Can I play the game offline?

Lives in Transit requires an internet connection to work. However, the game does not have any online game elements where your content is shared with other players.

Can I play the on mobile?

Unfortunately, Lives in Transit is developed for desktop browsers. The game may start on a mobile device, but the user interface may not work correctly.

How do I download Lives in Transit?

  • Lives in Transit
    • is played directly in the browser and does not require any download. Upon making an account, you can start playing.

    What are the system requirements for the game?

    The game requires an active internet connection and is developed for modern browsers. This means the latest versions of Chrome, Firefox, Internet Explorer/Edge and Safari are supported. Older versions and other browsers may work, but have not been tested.

    How do I save my game?

    The game is saved automatically as you play. Your sessions are always available in your user profile, no matter which machine you are on.

    What can I do if I'm stuck and nothing is happening?

    Some parts of the game might involve waiting a set amount of time before an event continues the story. If nothing is happening for a prolongued amount of time without the game story indicating it, you may be blocking pop-ups. Lives in Transit relies on pop-ups for the game to function.     Privacy

    How does Lives in Transit collect information from Visitors or Users?

    Lives in Transit requires the use of a database to function. This means all data users generate as part of the game is saved and tied to their account. If users log in through their university login, additional information like the user’s affiliation and home organization is collected in order to verify their login. This data is saved indefinitely unless you request a delete from us. The name you type in is assumed to be fictional, as is the text you generate as part of the game. Please do not include private or sensitive data in your answers.

    Does Lives in Transit create user profiles of everyone who visits the Site?

    No. We only generate a user profile when the user signs up through the sign-up form. Temporary cookies may be generated in order to make the site run smoothly. This cookie is automatically deleted when the user’s browser is closed. You can block the use of temporary cookies by adjusting the relevant settings in your web browser. Please note that this may cause Lives in Transit to stop functioning correctly. If you would like your account and data to be deleted, please contact us on Globalhistory@hist.uzh.ch. Accounts deleted this way will not be restored.

    What other parties can see me on Lives in Transit?

    The data Lives in Transit saves is not sent to any third parties. However, links and embedded websites (such as embedded Youtube videos) are subject to the respective website’s privacy policies, and interacting with these elements usually means you agree to their privacy policy. The game may request you to visit websites outside of the scope of Lives in Transit’s data protection. Lives in Transit cannot guarantee your privacy on external websites.

    When might Lives in Transit be required to disclose my personal information?

    We may disclose information about you if we are required to do so by law (such as a court order or a subpoena), or to prevent physical harm or financial loss or in connection with an investigation of suspected or actual illegal activity, or to protect or defend the rights or property of Lives in Transit, Users, Visitors, or others.

    What practices does Lives in Transit follow to secure my information?

    We follow standard industry practices to protect personal information, both during transmission and while we store it. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. If we discover a security incident that compromises your personal information, we will notify our users, in accordance with applicable law.

    Where can I send questions, comments or suggestions about Lives in Transit's privacy practices?

    Please contact us at Globalhistory@hist.uzh.ch if you have any questions regarding our privacy policy. If you would like to send us feedback about the game or report a bug, please contact us on our github page or our Twitter PLEASE DO NOT SEND ANY SENSITIVE INFORMATION TO US. Also, please note that we will need to verify and authenticate any emailed requests for access or changes to your personal or account information.
